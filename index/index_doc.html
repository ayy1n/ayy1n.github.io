RESEARCH

The first thing I did was look at successful portfolio/index sites and see what made them feel so engaging. I spent a lot of time on awwwards examining websites and noting down interesting interaction they had, and how they managed to make their websites responsive.

Since I was just going to create a simple one-page index file, and it didn't have to be super corporate-looking, I could have some fun with the layout (even if it wasn't optimal for accessibility).

WIREFRAME

I popped into Figma and quickly mocked up some wireframes for my site. I started with the mobile mockup, since I figured that it would be easier to adapt a mobile design to a desktop rather than vice versa.

UI STYLE GUIDE

For exercise 6, we had to put together a UI style guide. I did so with some general idea of what I wanted the overall site to look like, but without actually trying out different styles/colours on the Figma mockup. This was my mistake for not making a higher fidelity mockup, because in the end, my site turned out to look nothing like the UI style I had put together.

But it was still a good thing to try out, because now I know what to do in the future. Also, I used variables for the colourscheme, so changing things around wasn't that difficult. I think making the fonts variables as well would've been a good idea.

BASE SITE

I first made a basic working skeleton of the website, with divs in the right location and the correct spacing/padding. This looked very similar to the wireframe, with everything in black and white. From there I added some small interaction effects, including things like adding a bunch of hover effects (like shadows, so the div you're hovering over pops out), and having all buttons change color and scale up slightly.

RESPONSIVITY

One of the biggest challenges was making sure that the site worked well on mobile and adapted to a bunch of different screen sizes. I used mostly %-based div sizes so they would scale well, and made use of collapsing grids into single-column structures when the viewport was too small. All fonts got scaled down, and the biggest challenge of all was to get the navbar working as a hamburger menu.

I followed a tutorial on how to do so, and once I went over and reread the code a couple of times to ensure I actually understood what all of it was doing, I modified it to fit my needs. The changes I made include the way the hamburger menu slides open, the way the buttons are formatted, and a couple of tweaks so that the top navbar wouldn't disappear when I scrolled down.

There were a lot of small issues with padding/margins as I scaled things down, so fixing those involved a lot of trial and error. But after the site was mostly working the way I wanted it to, I simply filled in all the placeholders with actual content.

POLISH

To polish things up, I added a simple load-in animation, some basic parallax effectson the title, and tweaked the fonts/colours many, MANY times until it looked satisfactory. I added some hover filter effects so t hat hovering over buttons would make other divs apply/remove a filter through jQuery. I also added some more shadows for dimension, played around with the idea of having a div following the cursor (but that was very laggy), and I called it done :)
